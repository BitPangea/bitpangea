The
Truths

The Constitutional Realities of BitPangea

The Truths preserve what is unconditionally true of BitPangea. They do not instruct, persuade, or inspire conduct. They state the realities of the world as the world is understood within The Foundation.

Foundational Distinction

Truths describe what is. Principles guide what should be. Maxims preserve what should be remembered.

Purpose

The Truths are not rules, doctrines, aspirations, or values. They are constitutional realities: statements that remain true regardless of discovery, ownership, recognition, belief, or future interpretation.

Where The Codex defines terms, The Truths declare the conditions that make those definitions possible. Where The Principles will guide stewardship, The Truths remain descriptive and foundational.

I · Existence

Truth I

The world exists.

Existence precedes observation, discovery, ownership, or understanding.

Truth II

The world is one.

BitPangea is a single, continuous cartographic whole.

Truth III

The world is finite.

Its extent is complete, even where its details remain unknown.

Truth IV

The world cannot expand.

No later act can increase the world beyond its established extent.

Truth V

The world cannot be duplicated.

No copy, mirror, fork, or simulation becomes BitPangea itself.

II · Structure

Truth VI

Structure precedes discovery.

The world possesses order before anyone recognizes it.

Truth VII

Every Parcel belongs to the world.

No Parcel exists outside the single world to which it belongs.

Truth VIII

Every place has identity.

Coordinates identify place without possessing it.

Truth IX

Boundaries distinguish.

A Boundary creates distinction without creating a separate world.

Truth X

Relation is real.

No Parcel exists in isolation from the structure around it.

III · Permanence

Truth XI

The world remains.

The world persists beyond individual observers, records, claims, or generations.

Truth XII

Truth does not require recognition.

Reality exists independent of belief, approval, or awareness.

Truth XIII

The finite whole precedes every part.

Parcels, boundaries, and coordinates derive meaning from the world they belong to.

IV · Discovery

Truth XIV

Discovery reveals. It does not create.

To discover is to uncover what already exists.

Truth XV

The map follows the world.

The map records the world; it does not define the world into existence.

Truth XVI

Knowledge expands. The world remains.

Understanding may change without changing the world itself.

Truth XVII

The frontier is a condition of knowing.

What is unknown is not absent; it is unrevealed.

V · Record

Truth XVIII

The record preserves discovery.

What is discovered may be remembered through record.

Truth XIX

The Archive does not create the past.

Preservation carries memory forward; it does not invent what happened.

VI · Foundation

Truth XX

The Foundation preserves understanding.

The Foundation gathers the works through which the world becomes intelligible.

Truth XXI

BitPangea is one world.

All later structures, relations, records, and civilizations arise within the same finite world.

What The Truths Are Not

The Truths are not the ethical code of BitPangea. They are not the stewardship doctrine, the cultural sayings, or the civilizational constitution. Those belong elsewhere within The Foundation and future civilizational works.

The Truths are the floor beneath those works: the realities upon which later principles and maxims may stand.
