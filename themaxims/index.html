FOUNDATIONAL RECORD · ENDURING MAXIMS

The
Maxims

The Enduring Wisdom of BitPangea

The Maxims preserve the memorable expressions of BitPangea. They do not define the world and they do not instruct conduct. They distill what should be remembered, repeated, and carried forward.

Foundational Distinction

Truths describe what is. Principles guide what should be. Maxims preserve what should be remembered.

Purpose

The Maxims are the cultural sayings of The Foundation. They are short enough to be remembered, strong enough to be repeated, and broad enough to survive beyond the moment in which they were written.

Where The Truths are constitutional realities and The Principles are stewardship doctrine, The Maxims are the distilled wisdom of the world: phrases that carry BitPangea’s spirit without needing formal explanation.

I · The World

Maxim I

The world remains.

The simplest remembrance of BitPangea’s endurance.

Maxim II

One world. Many works.

A reminder that every foundational volume belongs to the same whole.

Maxim III

The foundation holds.

A saying for the works that make the world intelligible.

Maxim IV

The finite becomes meaningful.

Scarcity gives consequence to what would otherwise be endless.

II · Discovery

Maxim V

Discovery reveals.

What is found was not created by the act of finding.

Maxim VI

The map follows.

The world precedes every record made of it.

Maxim VII

Every frontier teaches.

The unknown is not empty; it is waiting to instruct.

Maxim VIII

The first mark matters.

Every beginning leaves a trace beyond itself.

III · Record

Maxim IX

The record remains.

What is preserved can outlast the hands that preserved it.

Maxim X

Maps remember.

A faithful map carries more than position; it carries history.

Maxim XI

Nothing preserved is entirely lost.

Preservation keeps meaning available to the future.

Maxim XII

Memory gives continuity.

A world without memory begins again too often.

IV · Relation

Maxim XIII

No parcel stands alone.

Every place is understood by what surrounds it.

Maxim XIV

Boundaries clarify.

Distinction can reveal without destroying relation.

Maxim XV

Between places, meaning gathers.

Relation is where structure begins to become world.

Maxim XVI

The path matters.

How one place leads to another is part of what the world becomes.

V · Stewardship

Maxim XVII

Stewardship remembers the future.

The world is carried for those who have not yet arrived.

Maxim XVIII

Build slowly where permanence is desired.

What is meant to endure deserves restraint.

Maxim XIX

Inheritance is responsibility.

What is handed down is not merely received; it is carried.

VI · Continuance

Maxim XX

Long after the builders, the world remains.

A reminder that the work may outlive those who shaped it.

Maxim XXI

Leave something worthy of return.

The measure of preservation is whether the future can come back and understand.

What The Maxims Are Not

The Maxims are not the constitutional realities of BitPangea. Those belong to The Truths. They are not stewardship doctrine. That belongs to The Principles. They are not Codex definitions, technical rules, or period records.

The Maxims are the memorable essence of The Foundation: sayings, reminders, and distilled wisdom that can be carried without carrying the entire library.
