Preservation

Before the Digital World could be explored, something had to move within it.

The Cursor gave position a visible form.

It crossed the surface of the screen.

It entered windows, followed paths, uncovered hidden places, and carried human intention into spaces without roads.

The Cursor did not create the world.

It did not carry the message.

It did not open the Window.

It wandered through what computation, communication, and interface had made possible.

With every movement, the unknown became reachable.

With every selection, possibility became direction.

The Digital World was no longer something humanity could only see.

It had become somewhere humanity could go.

The Cursor preserves more than a pointer.

It preserves the moment human intention became visible inside the machine.

A position could be chosen. A direction could be followed. A world could be explored.
