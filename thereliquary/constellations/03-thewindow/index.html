The Window

The opening through which humanity first looked into the Digital World.

Represents:

Preservation

Before the digital world became familiar, it had to be seen.

Before it could be explored, it needed an opening.

The Window transformed distance into visibility.

It allowed machines to become places.

It allowed information to become reachable.

It allowed people to look into systems they could not physically enter.

The Window did not compute.

It did not carry the message.

It revealed what computation and communication had made possible.

Invisible structures became visible worlds.

Hidden systems became places of interaction.

Long before digital civilization could be inhabited, humanity first needed a way to see it.
