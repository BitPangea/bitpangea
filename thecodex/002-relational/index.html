The Canonical Home of the Relational Domain

The Relational Domain preserves the foundational relational grammar through which the structural elements of BitPangea may be understood together. Its Codices describe objective relations that exist before ownership, governance, settlement, navigation, commerce, or civilization.

Constitutional Question
How do the elements within BitPangea relate?
Domain Doctrine Existence becomes understandable through relation.

Purpose

The Relational Domain establishes the first-order relational grammar through which structural concepts may be described in relation to one another.

It preserves relation, connection, measure, and orientation without assigning ownership, jurisdiction, access, value, purpose, movement, or civilizational meaning.

Relational Codices do not create the relations they describe. They preserve the canonical relational vocabulary through which those relations become formally understood.

Mode of Discovery

Relational inquiry proceeds through grammatical progression.

Each adopted Codex extends the relational language available to describe how Parcels and structural concepts may be understood together.

This progression does not imply that one relation creates another. It records the development of a complete first-order relational grammar.

The Relational Sequence

RL-I · June 2026

Adjacency

RL-II · June 2026

Contiguity

The cartographic condition by which two or more Parcels are connected through an unbroken path of Adjacencies.

RL-III · July 6, 2026

Distance

The cartographic measure describing the separation between two Parcels within BitPangea.

RL-IV · July 7, 2026

Direction

The cartographic orientation describing the relative position of one Parcel with respect to another.

What the Domain Establishes

  • Adjacency establishes direct cartographic relation.
  • Contiguity establishes connected relational structure.
  • Distance establishes measurable separation.
  • Direction establishes relative orientation.

First-Order Relational Grammar

Adjacency, Contiguity, Distance, and Direction together form the complete first-order relational grammar presently recognized within BitPangea.

These Codices establish the irreducible relational vocabulary through which structural concepts may be understood together.

Completion does not prohibit future relational discovery. It records that the foundational first-order grammar of relation has been constitutionally established.

Domain Distinction

Relation exists before civilization.

Ownership may assign meaning to relationships. Governance may regulate them. Navigation may traverse them. Settlement may occupy the structures they describe.

None of these create relation itself.
