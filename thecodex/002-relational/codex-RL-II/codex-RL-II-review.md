## Review Summary

Codex V underwent two rounds of constitutional review focused on both the proposed definition and its broader constitutional significance.

The first review cycle evaluated whether Contiguity represented a sufficiently distinct cartographic concept and whether it belonged within the foundational sequence of the Cartographic Period.

The second review cycle examined the constitutional implication that BitPangea itself constitutes a single Contiguity and whether that implication provides a cartographic explanation for the Declaration's principles of **One World** and **A Single Digital Supercontinent**.

## Principal Findings

This progression establishes:

| Codex | Concept                 |

---

### Adjacency Is Local. Contiguity Is Structural.

One of the most influential observations produced during review was:

> Adjacency is local. Contiguity is structural.

Adjacency describes the direct relationship between neighboring Parcels.

Contiguity describes the larger connected structure formed by many Adjacencies.

This distinction became foundational to the final understanding of Codex V.

---

### Path Replaced Chain

Early drafts proposed:

> An unbroken chain of Adjacencies

Reviewers noted that the word *chain* implied unnecessary linearity.

A continent may contain multiple routes and multiple connections simultaneously.

The adopted formulation became:

> An unbroken path of Adjacencies

This preserves connection without implying a single linear structure.

---

### Contiguity Explains One World

The most significant discovery of the review process was that Contiguity provides the first cartographic explanation for the Declaration's statement:

> One World

Prior to Codex V, the Declaration asserted that BitPangea was a single digital supercontinent.

After Codex V, that assertion acquired cartographic meaning.

BitPangea is one world because all Parcels participate within a single Contiguity.

---

### The World Remains Whole

Reviewers broadly agreed that the following constitutional implication should be preserved:

> BitPangea itself constitutes a single Contiguity.

This implication transformed Contiguity from a local cartographic concept into a world-scale constitutional principle.

---

### No Parcel Exists In Cartographic Isolation

A related conclusion emerged during the second review cycle:

> No Parcel exists in cartographic isolation.

Every Parcel participates in the greater structure of BitPangea.

This principle was ultimately adopted as a foundational doctrine of Codex V.
