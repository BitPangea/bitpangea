The Canonical Home of the World Properties Domain

The World Properties Domain preserves the canonical recognition of objective properties already possessed by The World. Its Codices do not create, grant, activate, or assemble those properties. They recognize and preserve what inquiry reveals.

Domain Question
What properties does the World possess?
Domain Doctrine The World possesses properties before those properties are discovered.

Purpose

The World Properties Domain exists to preserve objective properties of the already-existing World.

A World Property may remain unknown, unnamed, unmapped, or unrecorded without being absent. Discovery recognizes the property; it does not bring the property into existence.

Mode of Discovery

World Property Codices operate through ontological independence. No property must be created or activated by another.

Their ordering reflects priority of recognition: the principled order through which properties are most faithfully preserved.

Each class of World Properties follows its own natural mode of revelation.

World Property Categories

World Properties may be organized into categories according to the nature of the properties they preserve.

  • Categories are canonical divisions within the World Properties Domain.
  • Categories are not additional Domains.
  • Categories organize related World Property Codices according to the nature of the properties they preserve.
  • Categories emerge through discovery.
  • No category is presumed before its recognition.
  • The existence of Categories within this Domain does not imply that other Domains possess or require them.

Recognized Categories

At present, one category has been recognized within the World Properties Domain.

Spatial Properties preserve objective properties describing the spatial nature of The World.

  • WP-S-I — The Extent
  • WP-S-II — The Region
  • WP-S-III — The Cluster

Additional categories may be recognized only through future discovery.

Future Inquiry

The World Properties Domain remains open to further discovery.

Future Codices may recognize additional objective properties. Future inquiry may also reveal additional categories through which related properties can be faithfully organized.

Neither the number of World Properties nor the number of categories is predetermined.

World Property Doctrine The Codex does not decide what properties The World should possess. It preserves what The World reveals that it already possesses.
