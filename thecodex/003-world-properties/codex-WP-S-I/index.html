Domain Assignment

Codex WP-S-I belongs to the World Properties Domain (WP).

The World Properties Domain asks: What properties does the World possess?

World Property Codices preserve the constitutional recognition of objective properties already existing within The World. They do not create, assemble, grant, or activate those properties.

Unlike the constitutive progression of the Structural Domain or the grammatical progression of the Relational Domain, World Property Codices operate through ontological independence. A World Property exists regardless of whether another World Property has already been recognized.

Constitutional Transition

The Structural Domain established the constitutional vocabulary through which The World may be described.

The Relational Domain established the first-order grammar through which structural elements may be understood in relation to one another.

Codex WP-S-I begins a different mode of inquiry. The World Properties Domain recognizes objective properties possessed by the already-existing World. Its Codices follow their natural mode of revelation rather than a required chain of logical construction.

Mode of Discovery

World Properties exist prior to their discovery, naming, mapping, or codification.

The order of World Property Codices therefore reflects constitutional priority of recognition: the order in which the properties are most faithfully and naturally preserved through constitutional inquiry.

This ordering is principled but not creative. Recognition neither produces a property nor makes its existence dependent upon a previous Codex.

The Definition

The World is finite and possesses spatial scope whether or not that scope has been measured, mapped, or observed.

Extent identifies that objective scope without prescribing its geometry, representation, measurement, implementation, or interpretation.

Extent establishes no ownership, governance, jurisdiction, settlement, access, value, movement, purpose, or civilizational meaning.

Formal Definition

An Extent is the cartographic property describing the spatial scope of the World within BitPangea.

Extent belongs to The World as a whole. It is not a property of any individual Parcel, Boundary, Coordinate, relation, inhabitant, claim, institution, or settlement.

This Codex constitutionally recognizes that The World possesses spatial scope. It does not determine how that scope must be measured, represented, mapped, or implemented.

Immutable Properties

Every Extent possesses the following immutable properties:

World Property

Extent is an objective property possessed by The World as a whole.

Spatial Scope

Extent describes the cartographic scope of The World.

Recognition

Extent is recognized rather than created. Discovery, naming, mapping, or codification does not bring it into existence.

Independence

Extent exists regardless of whether any Region, Cluster, or other World Property has been constitutionally recognized.

Permanence

Inquiry or representation does not expand, contract, duplicate, or otherwise alter the Extent of The World.

Neutrality

Extent assigns no ownership, jurisdiction, governance, settlement, access, value, purpose, or civilizational meaning.

Principles Established by Codex WP-S-I

1. The World Possesses Properties

The World possesses objective properties that may be constitutionally recognized without being created by the Codex.

2. World Properties Are Recognized, Not Created

Inquiry, discovery, naming, mapping, and codification preserve recognition of World Properties but do not produce them.

3. World Properties Exist Prior to Discovery

A World Property remains real regardless of whether it has been observed, understood, named, mapped, or recorded.

4. World Properties Operate Through Ontological Independence

No World Property must be created or constitutionally activated by the Codex that precedes it.

5. Ordering Reflects Constitutional Priority of Recognition

The numbering of World Property Codices preserves a principled order of constitutional recognition rather than a chain of logical construction.

6. Spatial Scope Does Not Imply Civilizational Meaning

Extent establishes no ownership, administration, settlement, governance, access, value, movement, purpose, privilege, or use.

Scope

This Codex recognizes Extent as the first Spatial Property within the World Properties Domain.

It establishes that The World possesses spatial scope. It does not establish:

  • The geometry or representation of the World's outer limit
  • The method by which spatial scope is measured
  • The nature of any surface, medium, edge, or topology
  • The number, boundaries, or character of Regions or Clusters
  • The criteria governing any future World Property
  • Ownership, claims, governance, commerce, settlement, culture, value, or purpose
  • Cartographic implementation

These matters remain subjects for future constitutional inquiry.

Constitutional Sequence

Codex WP-S-I marks the beginning of constitutional property inquiry.

The Structural Domain establishes structural vocabulary. The Relational Domain establishes relational grammar. The World Properties Domain recognizes objective properties possessed by The World.

Within the World Properties Domain, Extent is recognized first by constitutional priority. Its recognition neither creates later properties nor makes their existence dependent upon it.
