# Codex WP-I — The Extent

## Definition

> **An Extent is the cartographic property describing the spatial scope of the World within BitPangea.**

---

# Domain Assignment

Codex WP-I belongs to the **World Properties Domain (WP)**.

The World Properties Domain asks:

> **What properties does the World possess?**

Unlike the Structural Domain, which establishes what exists, or the Relational Domain, which establishes how Parcels relate, the World Properties Domain examines the properties already possessed by the World itself.

The first category within the World Properties Domain is **Spatial Properties**, recognizing that the spatial character of the World naturally follows the completion of the Structural and Relational Domains.

---

# Constitutional Function

Codex WP-I does not define the World.

Codex WP-I establishes **Extent** as the first recognized property of the World.

Extent is not a structural primitive.

Extent is not a relational concept.

Extent is a property possessed by the World itself.

The World precedes the recognition of its properties.

The World Properties Domain describes those properties; it does not create, assemble, or modify them.

---

# Formal Recognition

The World possesses an Extent.

Extent describes the World's spatial scope without assigning measurement systems, dimensional units, coordinate systems, geometry, ownership, governance, or civilizational meaning.

Extent is recognized as a property of the World itself rather than a consequence of observation or discovery.

---

# Foundational Doctrine

The World Properties Domain examines properties possessed by the World itself.

Some properties are intrinsic to the World.

Other properties become constitutionally recognizable through the relational grammar established by the Relational Domain.

The purpose of the World Properties Domain is to describe those properties rather than to create or assemble them.

---

# Immutable Properties

The constitutional relationship between the World and its Extent possesses the following immutable properties.

---

## Wholeness

Extent describes the World as a single continuous cartographic whole.

---

## Universality

Every portion of the World participates within the same Extent.

No valid cartographic structure exists beyond it.

---

## Permanence

Discovery, mapping, observation, or codification do not expand, reduce, or otherwise alter the World's Extent.

---

## Neutrality

Extent assigns no ownership, governance, jurisdiction, accessibility, movement, settlement, value, or purpose.

It remains purely cartographic.

---

## Priority

The World's Extent exists prior to observation, measurement, description, mapping, or constitutional recognition.

---

## Independence

Extent exists independently of any particular coordinate system, measurement methodology, implementation, or cartographic representation.

---

# What Extent Is

- A property of the World.
- The spatial scope of the World.
- A constitutional recognition of the World's cartographic reach.
- The first Spatial Property within the World Properties Domain.
- A property describing the World as a whole.

---

- A Direction.
- A Region.
- A Cluster.
- A Locality.
- A civilizational construct.
- A measurement system.

---

# Principles Established by Codex WP-I

## 1. The World Possesses Properties

The World possesses properties independent of constitutional recognition.

The Codex describes those properties; it does not originate them.

---

## 2. Extent Is a Property of the Whole

Extent belongs to the World itself rather than to any individual Parcel or collection of Parcels.

---

## 3. Extent Precedes Spatial Recognition

Recognition of Regions, Clusters, Localities, and future Spatial Properties occurs within the World's already existing Extent.

---

## 4. Extent Is Constitutionally Neutral

Extent assigns no physical, political, economic, civilizational, or environmental meaning.

It remains purely cartographic.

---

## 5. Relational Grammar Enables Recognition

The Relational Domain establishes the constitutional grammar through which higher-order spatial properties become recognizable.

Extent itself does not depend upon those relationships for its existence.

---

## 6. World Properties Reveal Rather Than Assemble

World Properties are discovered through constitutional inquiry.

They are not assembled from Parcels, nor constructed through relational combinations.

---

# Scope

This Codex establishes Extent as the first recognized Spatial Property of the World.

It does not establish:

- The character of the World's Surface.
- The nature of the World's outer Limit.
- Measurement systems.
- Units of distance or area.
- Coordinate implementations.
- Regional classifications.
- Cluster recognition.
- Locality recognition.
- Physical, environmental, or temporal properties.
- Civilizational interpretation.

These subjects remain within future constitutional inquiry.

---

# Cartographic Doctrine

> **The World exists.  
> Structural inquiry reveals what exists.  
> Relational inquiry reveals how what exists relates.  
> World Properties reveal what is true about the World itself.**
