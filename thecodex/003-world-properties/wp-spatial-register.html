Spatial Properties

Spatial Properties preserve the objective spatial properties of The World. Each property exists prior to constitutional recognition and is preserved through discovery rather than creation.

WP-I · Extent

The cartographic property describing the spatial scope of the World.

WP-II · Region

The cartographic property describing a coherent large-scale portion of the World.

WP-III · Cluster

The cartographic property describing a contiguous portion of the World.

Spatial Property Codices are ordered by constitutional priority of recognition. Their sequence does not imply that one property creates, causes, or constitutionally activates another.
