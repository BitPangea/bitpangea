# Codex WP-II — The Region

**Domain:** World Properties (WP)  
**Category:** Spatial Properties  
**Codex:** WP-II  
**Adopted:** July 10, 2026

---

# Definition

> **A Region is the cartographic property describing a coherent large-scale portion of the World within BitPangea.**

---

# Domain Assignment

Codex WP-II belongs to the **World Properties Domain (WP)**.

The World Properties Domain asks:

> **What properties does the World possess?**

Unlike the Structural Domain, which establishes what exists, or the Relational Domain, which establishes how existing things relate, the World Properties Domain preserves intrinsic properties already possessed by the World itself.

Region belongs to this Domain because it describes an inherent spatial property recognized within the World rather than a structural object or relational concept.

---

# Constitutional Progression

Codex WP-I established **Extent** as the spatial scope of the World.

Codex WP-II proceeds inward from that scope.

Once the World is recognized as possessing Extent, constitutional inquiry may recognize coherent large-scale portions existing within that Extent.

Region therefore does not divide the World into existence.

It describes large-scale spatial organization already possessed by the World.

---

# Spatial Properties

Region is the second adopted Codex within the **Spatial Properties** category of the World Properties Domain.

Current constitutional progression:

- **WP-I — The Extent**
- **WP-II — The Region**

Together these establish the first adopted Spatial Properties of BitPangea.

---

# Constitutional Meaning

The Extent of the World makes regional recognition possible.

A Region exists not to establish ownership, jurisdiction, governance, settlement, value, access, purpose, or civilizational identity.

Instead, it describes a coherent large-scale portion of the World.

A Region may be recognized through spatial coherence without requiring a constitutionally prescribed size, shape, boundary, population, function, or use.

---

# Formal Definition

A Region is the cartographic property describing a coherent large-scale portion of the World within BitPangea.

A Region is a property of the World rather than a property of any individual Parcel, inhabitant, institution, government, settlement, or claim.

This Codex establishes that coherent large-scale portions may be recognized within the World.

It does not prescribe how coherence is measured, how boundaries are represented, or how Regions are named or mapped.

---

# Immutable Properties

Every Region possesses the following immutable properties.

## World Property

A Region is recognized within the World and belongs to its spatial character rather than to any individual Parcel or institution.

---

## Coherence

A Region is recognized as a portion of the World whose spatial character allows it to be understood as belonging together.

---

## Large-Scale Character

A Region describes large-scale spatial organization without requiring a fixed constitutional threshold.

---

## Variability

A Region possesses no constitutionally prescribed:

- Size
- Shape
- Geometry
- Boundary
- Number of Parcels

---

## Recognition

A Region is recognized rather than created.

Discovery, mapping, naming, or codification does not bring a Region into existence.

---

## Neutrality

A Region assigns no:

- Ownership
- Governance
- Jurisdiction
- Settlement
- Access
- Value
- Purpose
- Civilizational meaning

---

# What a Region Is

A Region is:

- A cartographic property
- A coherent large-scale portion of the World
- A Spatial Property recognized within Extent
- A large-scale expression of spatial organization
- A property recognized rather than created

---

# What a Region Is Not

A Region is not:

- A separate continent
- A political territory
- A governmental jurisdiction
- An administrative district
- A settlement
- A claim of ownership
- A fixed geometry
- A prescribed boundary
- A statement of value, purpose, culture, or use

---

# Principles Established by Codex WP-II

## 1. Extent Makes Region Possible

Regions are recognized within the spatial scope of the World.

Without Extent, no large-scale portion of the World can be constitutionally situated.

---

## 2. Regions Are Recognized, Not Created

Classification, naming, discovery, mapping, or codification may reveal a Region.

They do not create it.

---

## 3. Coherence Does Not Require Uniformity

A Region may contain internal differences while remaining coherent as a large-scale portion of the World.

---

## 4. Region Does Not Require Contiguity

Spatial coherence may exist without requiring every Parcel within a Region to participate in a single uninterrupted Contiguity.

---

## 5. Scale Does Not Establish Authority

The large-scale character of a Region grants no ownership, governance, jurisdiction, privilege, control, or right.

---

## 6. Region Precedes Civilizational Meaning

A Region exists as a property of the World before any culture, institution, settlement, government, or inhabitant assigns meaning to it.

---

# Relation to Future Spatial Inquiry
