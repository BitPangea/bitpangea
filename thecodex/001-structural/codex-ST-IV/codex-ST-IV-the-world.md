# Codex ST-IV — The World

**Adopted:** June 21, 2026
**Period:** Structural Domain

---

## Definition

The World is recognized within the Structural Domain as the singular, continuous cartographic whole declared by BitPangea.

---

## The Definition

The Declaration establishes:

> The World is the singular, continuous cartographic whole within BitPangea inside which all structural units, distinctions, and references exist.

Codex ST-IV does not define The World.

Instead, this Codex establishes the constitutional relationship between The World and all Structural concepts.

Parcels, Boundaries, Coordinates, and all future Structural concepts exist within The World.

Structural inquiry reveals the structure of The World.

It does not create it.

---

## Formal Definition

The World is the singular, continuous cartographic whole within which all Structural concepts exist, participate, and derive meaning.

The World precedes Structural inquiry.

Structural inquiry occurs within The World.

---

## Immutable Properties

The World possesses the following immutable properties:

### Singularity

There exists one World.

All Structural concepts participate within the same cartographic whole.

### Continuity

The World remains continuous regardless of complexity, discovery, mapping, or future development.

### Containment

All Structural concepts exist within The World.

No Structural concept possesses independent existence beyond it.

### Priority

The World precedes observation, mapping, description, codification, and discovery.

Structural inquiry reveals what already exists.

### Permanence

The World is not created, expanded, reduced, or altered through discovery.

Discovery reveals structure.

It does not create structure.

### Unity

The World remains a single cartographic whole regardless of the number of Parcels, Boundaries, Coordinates, or future Structural concepts recognized within it.

* The singular cartographic whole
* The context within which Structural concepts exist
* The object of Structural inquiry
* The foundation of cartographic permanence
* The source of Structural unity

## What The World Is Not

* A Parcel
* A Boundary
* A Coordinate
* A Claim
* A Settlement
* A Government
* A Civilizational construct
* A product of discovery
* A consequence of mapping

---

## Foundational Principles

### 1. Structural Concepts Exist Within The World

Parcels, Boundaries, Coordinates, and all future Structural concepts exist within The World.

They do not create it.

### 2. Structural Concepts Derive Meaning Through The World

No Structural concept can be fully understood in isolation.

Each derives meaning through participation in the singular whole.

### 3. Structural Discovery Does Not Create The World

Discovery reveals what already exists within The World.

Discovery is an act of recognition rather than creation.

### 4. Structural Discovery Does Not Expand The World

The World remains fixed regardless of future inquiry.

Discovery may increase knowledge of The World.

It does not enlarge it.

### 5. The World Contains All Structural Concepts

All present and future Structural concepts participate within the same cartographic whole.

### 6. The World Precedes Structural Inquiry

The World exists prior to its observation, description, mapping, or codification.

Structural inquiry begins within a world that already exists.

## Scope

This Codex recognizes The World within the Structural Domain and establishes the constitutional relationship between The World and Structural concepts.

It does not establish:

* Surface
* Limit
* Discovery procedures
* Observation procedures
* Cartographic implementation
* Civilizational meaning

These questions remain subjects for future inquiry.
