Lineage Principle The Luminaries preserves not those who were admired, but those without whom the next idea could not have existed.

The Lineage

The Lineage traces a path from abstract mathematics to logic, computation, information, cryptography, digital cash, programmable agreements, and finally decentralized digital scarcity.

Each preserved discovery made the next discovery more possible.

Lineage I

Binary

Preserved through Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz

Binary established the symbolic foundation of the digital world: the reduction of representation into the language of 1 and 0.

Lineage II

Logic

Preserved through George Boole

Logic became mathematical, allowing thought, truth, and decision to be expressed through symbolic operations.

Lineage III

Computation

Preserved through Alan Turing

Computation became universal, revealing that a machine could execute any describable algorithm.

Lineage IV

Information

Preserved through Claude Shannon

Information became measurable, transforming communication, circuits, signals, and data into a formal science.

Lineage V

Public-Key Cryptography

Preserved through Whitfield Diffie and Martin Hellman

Secure communication no longer required a shared secret in advance, opening the path toward digital signatures, trustless verification, and sovereign digital identity.

Lineage VI

Digital Cash

Preserved through David Chaum

Cryptographic money became imaginable, introducing privacy, electronic cash, and the earliest structures of digital value.

Lineage VII

Programmable Agreements

Preserved through Nick Szabo

Agreements, property, and economic relationships became programmable, preparing the conceptual ground for trust-minimized digital systems.

Lineage VIII

Digital Scarcity

Preserved through Satoshi Nakamoto · Name Unknown

Digital scarcity was demonstrated without central authority, changing the meaning of ownership, permanence, verification, and trust in the digital age.
